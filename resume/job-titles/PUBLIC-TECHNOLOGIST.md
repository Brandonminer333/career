## What this role is

A **Public Technologist** (public interest technologist, civic technologist) applies data and software to **government accountability, civil liberties, privacy, and equitable public services**. The field — promoted by Ford Foundation’s Public Interest Tech network, Code for America, EFF, ACLU-type orgs, and university PIT programs — combines **technical craft with policy and advocacy**, measuring success by **impact on people and institutions**, not revenue.

Roles appear at nonprofits, digital service teams, think tanks, journalism, and “tech for good” vendors. Work spans **responsible AI, data advocacy, secure handling of sensitive data, and making public systems legible**.

Overlaps with **Data Scientist** (policy analysis, public datasets), **AI Engineer** (ML for oversight/automation), and **Forward Deployed Engineer** (embedding with legal/advocacy staff on secure infra).

**Distinct emphasis vs siblings:** **mission, rights, privacy, and public accountability** over generic Silicon Valley ML product language.

| Priority | Signals |

**Typical stack:** Python, SQL, GIS/geospatial (Plotly, OSM), dashboards, private/secure deploy vs default public cloud.
