## What this role is

A **Data Scientist** turns messy data into **decisions and predictive systems** — problem framing, EDA, statistical inference, ML modeling, and communication to stakeholders. In 2025, the role still spends heavy time on **data prep and pipeline work** (often 60–80% of the job) but is judged on **rigor, reproducibility, and actionable insight**, not just notebook experiments.

Overlaps with **AI Engineer** (NLP/classification pipelines), **Forward Deployed Engineer** (delivering analysis to embedded stakeholders), and **Public Technologist** (policy/civic datasets and public impact).

**Distinct emphasis vs siblings:** **statistics, experimentation, and quantified findings** over pure infra narrative or GenAI product hype.

## What employers look for (2025–2026 market)

| Priority | Signals |
|----------|---------|
| **Statistical rigor** | Regression, mixed models, multicollinearity, hypothesis thinking, experimental design |
| **ML application** | Classification, NLP, feature engineering, imbalanced data, model validation |
| **Data engineering fluency** | SQL, ETL, cleaning heterogeneous sources — increasingly expected |
| **End-to-end ownership** | From raw data to recommendations dashboards or model outputs |
| **Communication** | Dashboards, briefings, quantified business/policy outcomes |

**Typical stack:** Python, R, SQL, Pandas/Scikit-learn, PyTorch/TensorFlow as needed, Streamlit/Tableau, cloud notebooks + some deployment awareness.
